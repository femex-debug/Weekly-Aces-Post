TIPFemi Tip of the Day10:00 AM
Week 6 tip and this one is about something most people overlook completely 💡 Your split step. Every time your opponent is about to make contact with the ball you should be doing a small hop to get on the balls of your feet. Not a big jump. Just a tiny bounce so your weight is loaded and you can explode in either direction. Most recreational players stand flat-footed waiting to see where the ball goes and then they are already late. The split step fixes that. It is the single movement that separates reactive players from proactive ones. Try it tonight on every single point and watch how much faster you feel on the court 🎾 - Femi

TIPFemi Tip of the Day10:00 AM
Something I have been watching lately 💡 When you are at the net a lot of players back up when the ball comes hard at them. Do the opposite. Step into it. A short punch volley from close range is way more effective than a big swing from behind the service line. The net is your friend. Own it when you are up there. Do not retreat 🎾 - Femi
CHALLENGEWeek 6 Challenge11:00 AM
🎯 WEEK 6 CHALLENGE - INSIDE OUT This week is about shot selection. Your goal is to play an entire game using ONLY inside out forehands on any ball that lands in your backhand corner. No backhand at all on those balls - you have to run around it and hit the forehand. This forces you to work on footwork, court positioning and shot discipline all at once. It is uncomfortable but that is the point. Advanced players - try this in a full set not just a game 🎾 ✅ Complete it = 5 bonus ranking points 📸 Catch one of those inside out forehands on camera and post it here! Who is brave enough? TIPFemi Tip of the Day10:00 AM
This one is about serving pressure 💡 Most recreational players only ever practice their first serve. So when it goes in they have no idea what to do with the advantage. Start thinking about your second serve as a weapon not a safety shot. Try adding some topspin to your second serve so it kicks up high to your opponent's backhand. A high kicking second serve to the backhand is one of the hardest balls to attack in tennis. Work on it tonight. Your second serve should scare people not give them a free point 🎾 - Femi

TIPFemi Tip of the Day10:00 AM
Thursday tip - this one is for the competitive players 💡 Start tracking your unforced errors. After every practice session try to remember roughly how many times you missed a ball that you should have made with no pressure on you. Not the hard shots. The easy ones you just put in the net or long. Most recreational players lose matches not because their opponent beats them but because they beat themselves. If you can cut your unforced errors in half you will win a lot more matches without changing anything else about your game 🎾 - Femi
