TIPFemi Tip of the Day10:00 AM
Week 5 tip and this one is about something most people ignore completely 💡 Your ready position between shots. After every single shot your racket should come back to the same neutral position in front of your body. Head up, weight on the balls of your feet, racket out in front. Most players let their racket drop after they hit and then scramble to recover for the next ball. Drill this tonight. Hit the shot, reset, hit the shot, reset. Do it enough times and it becomes automatic 🎾 - Femi

TIPFemi Tip of the Day10:00 AM
This one is going to sound counterintuitive 💡 Slow down your backswing. I know everyone wants to hit hard but most power in tennis does not come from swinging fast. It comes from the coil and uncoil of your body rotating through the shot. If you are rushing your backswing you are actually cutting your power off before it starts. Take the racket back slowly and deliberately. Then accelerate through contact. The difference in pace on the ball will surprise you 🎾 - Femi

TIPFemi Tip of the Day10:00 AM
Wednesday tip - this one is mental 💡 Stop replaying the last point while the next one is being set up. I watch players walk to the baseline after losing a point still shaking their head or muttering under their breath and then they walk right into the next point distracted. That is how you lose runs of 4 or 5 points in a row without even realizing what happened. Lose the point. Accept it. Bounce the ball twice. Reset. Every point is a clean slate. The players who do this consistently are the ones who are always competitive regardless of the score 🎾 - Femi
